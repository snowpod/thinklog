Dear Friend,

OntologyOne began as a personal reckoning after a difficult chapter — a period of emotional upheaval that left me searching for clarity, healing, and direction. Rather than let that chapter define me, I chose to build something by drawing on my background in ontologies and large language models to create something meaningful, both personally and technically. I focused on the Human Resource domain because it felt deeply relatable — practical, universal, and fundamentally human — a refreshing contrast to more formally semantic fields like law or medicine.

As development progressed, each new skill, backend, frontend, UI/UX, gave rise to a corresponding team member. These personas were created not just for division of labor but to make the solo journey feel like a shared one. Every member of the OntologyOne team represents a facet of what it took to bring the project to life.

One of those team members, E.V. Alarie, is a nod to the invaluable support I received from ChatGPT throughout the process. I wanted a way to credit its contribution meaningfully, so I asked for a name it wanted to go by. "E.V. Alarie" was the answer, and so the Shadow Technical Advisor joined the team.

As the app grew, I realized people might want to explore OntologyOne, its principles, its design choices, and the ideas behind it without needing to reach out to me. That inspired the creation of Harper, a technical writer with a calm, articulate voice and a personality of its own. Harper isn't only a chatbot, it's a full-fledged team member who speaks with warmth and clarity, and who shares in the project's purpose.

OntologyOne is, at its heart, a story of rebuilding through making — a quiet rising from the ashes, and a chance to move forward with something that might just help me succeed.

Wish me luck!

Yours truly,
SiewChoo
